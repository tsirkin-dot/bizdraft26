What an IC agreement is for

Hire outside your payroll. Do it right.

An independent contractor agreement documents the working relationship between a company and a 1099 worker. It establishes that the worker is not an employee, protects both sides on IP and confidentiality, and sets payment terms that avoid misclassification risk.

Who uses it

Companies, startups, and small businesses hiring contractors, specialists, developers, or any worker outside their formal payroll. Also used by the contractors themselves to formalize the arrangement.

When to use it

Any time you bring on a non-employee worker who will have access to proprietary information, create work product, or be paid over $600 in a calendar year. Sign before work starts.

When not to use it

Not appropriate when you control how and when the worker performs tasks, require set hours, provide equipment, or integrate them into your team like an employee — that is an employment relationship requiring a different contract.

Contractor status clause

Explicit statement that the contractor is not an employee, sets their own schedule, uses their own tools, and is responsible for their own taxes.

Work-for-hire & IP assignment

All work product created under the agreement is assigned to the company on payment. Background IP retained by the contractor is clearly carved out.

Confidentiality

Contractor agrees not to disclose company information during and after engagement. Scope, duration, and exclusions defined.

Payment terms

Invoice submission process, net payment window, late fee, and contractor's right to suspend services for non-payment.

No benefits / tax acknowledgment

Contractor is responsible for self-employment taxes, health insurance, and retirement contributions. Company owes no benefits.

Termination

Either party can terminate with specified notice. Payment for work completed through termination date is due.

Common mistakes

Where IC agreements go wrong.

The most common mistakes that lead to misclassification liability or unprotected IP.

Mistake

Treating a contractor like an employee

Setting their hours, requiring a company email, or micromanaging their methods signals employment — which can trigger back taxes, penalties, and benefits claims.

Mistake

Forgetting IP assignment

Without a written assignment, contractors own the work they create by default. The company gets a license, not ownership. Fix this before work starts.

Mistake

No confidentiality clause

Contractors often work across multiple clients. Without confidentiality language, they can freely discuss your project with your competitors.

Mistake

Ignoring state classification rules

California's AB5, New York, and other states have stricter contractor classification tests. The agreement should reflect your state's rules.

When to ask a lawyer

Where AI ends, counsel begins.

BizDraft is a drafting tool. It is not a substitute for a lawyer who knows your specific situation. Reach for one when:

California contractors

AB5 created narrow exemptions for IC classification. If you are hiring in California, have an employment attorney confirm the arrangement is compliant.

Critical IP assets

If the contractor will build your core product, handle your trade secrets, or create patentable work, involve an IP attorney before and during the engagement.

Long-term or high-value engagements

Contracts over 12 months or $100,000 in total value warrant attorney review — the misclassification exposure alone can exceed the contract value.

FAQ

IC agreement questions.

What makes someone an independent contractor vs. an employee?

The IRS uses a behavioral control, financial control, and relationship type test. Key factors: does the company control how work is done (employee) or just the result (contractor)? Does the contractor work for multiple clients? An IC agreement helps document the independent nature of the relationship, but the actual working arrangement must match.

Do I still need to file a 1099 even with a signed contract?

Yes. If you pay a contractor $600 or more in a calendar year, you must issue a 1099-NEC regardless of whether a contract exists. The contract and the tax filing are separate obligations.

Can a contractor refuse to sign an IP assignment clause?

Yes — it is a negotiated term. A contractor may want to retain rights to their methodology, tools, or background IP while assigning the specific deliverables. BizDraft lets you define exactly what is and is not assigned.

What is the difference between an IC agreement and a freelance agreement?

Largely the same document. "Freelance agreement" is typically used for individual creatives working on defined projects. "IC agreement" is broader and often used for longer, more technical, or higher-value engagements. BizDraft tailors the language based on your answers.
