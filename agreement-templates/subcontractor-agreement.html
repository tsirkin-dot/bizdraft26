What a subcontractor agreement is for

Delegate work. Keep the client relationship.

A subcontractor agreement governs the relationship between a prime contractor and a subcontractor delivering part of the prime's obligations to an end client. It flows down the relevant terms from the prime contract, defines payment timing, allocates IP, requires insurance, and prevents the sub from going around the prime to the client.

Who uses it

Agencies outsourcing specialized work (development, translation, photography) to subs. General contractors subbing out trade work. Any prime contractor who brings in help to deliver on a client contract.

When to use it

Every time you bring in a subcontractor on a client engagement. Without a sub agreement, the sub has no confidentiality obligations, no IP assignment, and no prohibition on going direct to your client.

When not to use it

Not appropriate for direct client relationships — the sub works for you, not the end client. If the sub will interact directly with the end client, structure it as a separate engagement with a service agreement.

Common mistakes

Where subcontractor agreements fail.

The most common ways prime contractors get burned by unprotected sub relationships.

Mistake

No non-solicitation clause

Without a non-solicitation clause, nothing stops the sub from going directly to your client after the engagement ends — cutting you out of future work you introduced them to.

Mistake

No IP assignment

If the sub owns what they built, you cannot fulfill your IP assignment obligation to your end client. Sub IP must be assigned to you on payment.

Mistake

Pay-when-paid without a cap

Unlimited pay-when-paid terms in some states are unenforceable. Specify a maximum waiting period before payment is due regardless of whether the prime has been paid by the end client.

Mistake

No insurance requirement

If an uninsured sub causes harm to the client's data or property, the prime contractor may be on the hook. Require adequate insurance and get a certificate of insurance before work begins.

When to ask a lawyer

Where AI ends, counsel begins.

BizDraft is a drafting tool. It is not a substitute for a lawyer who knows your specific situation. Reach for one when:

Government or regulated contracts

Federal and state government contracts have mandatory flow-down requirements (FAR, DFARS) that must be included in subcontracts. Attorney review is required.

Construction subcontracts

Construction sub agreements have specialized mechanics lien, pay-when-paid, and retainage rules that vary by state. Use a construction-specific attorney for these.

Large sub engagements

Subcontracts over $100,000 or involving critical deliverables for enterprise clients warrant attorney review of indemnification, insurance requirements, and liability allocation.

FAQ

Subcontractor agreement questions.

What does "flow-down" mean in a subcontract?

Flow-down means certain terms from the prime contract are incorporated into the sub agreement and bind the subcontractor as if they were a direct party to the prime contract. Common flow-down terms include confidentiality, IP assignment, and compliance with client policies.

What is pay-when-paid and is it enforceable?

Pay-when-paid means the prime pays the sub only after the end client pays the prime. Enforceability varies by state — some states treat it as a timing clause only (payment is delayed, not contingent), others enforce the contingency. To be safe, include a maximum waiting period even with pay-when-paid language.

Does the sub know who the end client is?

Sometimes, sometimes not — and for non-solicitation purposes, it matters. The non-solicitation clause is most effective when it names the specific client. If the prime wants to keep the client identity confidential, the clause needs to be drafted with a description rather than a name.

Can a sub use sub-subcontractors?

Only if the sub agreement allows it. If the sub agreement is silent, the sub can generally delegate performance but remains responsible for it. If the prime needs to approve further sub-delegation, that approval requirement should be explicit in the agreement.
