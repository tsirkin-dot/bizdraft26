What an NDA is for

Keep something private. In writing.

An NDA — non-disclosure agreement — is the document you sign before sharing information that shouldn't leave the conversation. It defines what's confidential, for how long, and what the recipient can and can't do with it.

Who uses it

Founders pitching investors. Vendors quoting on a project. Employees seeing trade secrets. Partners exploring a deal. Anyone about to share something that matters.

When to use it

Before the pitch deck goes out. Before the demo. Before the consulting call where you'll show the data. NDAs work in the time window between "I'd like to tell you" and "here's the thing."

When not to use it

For information that's already public, for routine business chats with vendors you've worked with for years, or when the other side has legitimate reasons to share with their team or counsel — NDAs don't override that.

Common mistakes

Where NDAs fall apart.

The most common reasons an NDA doesn't actually protect the information it was supposed to.

Mistake

Defining "confidential" too broadly

If everything is confidential, nothing is enforceable. Specific scopes hold up better in court.

Mistake

No purpose limitation

Without a use-limit clause, the recipient can use your info for anything — just not share it.

Mistake

Indefinite duration

NDAs with no end date are unenforceable in many states. Three to five years is common; perpetual rarely is.

Mistake

No carve-out for compelled disclosure

If the recipient is served a subpoena, the NDA needs to allow legally compelled disclosure with notice.

Mistake

One-way when it should be mutual

If both sides will share sensitive info, a one-way NDA leaves one side exposed.

FAQ

NDA-specific questions.

Is an AI-generated NDA legally binding?

A signed NDA is binding under the same rules as any signed agreement — both parties competent, clear terms, lawful purpose, signatures. The drafting tool doesn't change that. For high-stakes situations, have a lawyer review the Word file before signing.

Can I edit the NDA after it's generated?

Yes. Every clause is editable. Accept BizDraft's suggested values, adjust them, or rewrite a clause entirely. The downloaded Word file is fully editable in any word processor.

Can I use this NDA with clients, investors, employees?

Yes for all three, though the right shape differs. Client and investor NDAs are usually mutual or one-way. Employee NDAs are a separate template (linked from the catalog) with employment-specific clauses.
