What a consulting agreement is for

Your expertise. A clear engagement.

A consulting agreement governs advisory relationships — engagements where a consultant provides specialized knowledge, recommendations, and analysis rather than just executing tasks. It protects both sides on deliverables, scope of advice, conflicts, and payment.

Who uses it

Management consultants, strategy advisors, financial consultants, HR consultants, IT consultants, and any expert hired for their knowledge and judgment rather than hands-on execution.

When to use it

Before the first advisory session, strategy call, or deliverable. Particularly important when the consultant will be involved in sensitive decisions, see confidential financials, or advise on competitive strategy.

When not to use it

When the work is primarily hands-on execution with defined output (use a service agreement), or when the relationship is with a board-level advisor receiving equity compensation (use an advisor agreement).

Common mistakes

Where consulting agreements fall short.

The most common gaps that create disputes in advisory engagements.

Mistake

No advice-limitation clause

Without it, the consultant can be held responsible for business outcomes based on their recommendations. Advisory work needs a clear disclaimer that decisions rest with the client.

Mistake

No conflict-of-interest disclosure

If the consultant later works with a competitor and no conflict clause exists, the client has limited recourse. Require ongoing disclosure from day one.

Mistake

Retainer without scope limits

An open-ended retainer with no included-hours definition invites the client to treat the consultant as a full-time employee. Define the monthly commitment.

Mistake

Vague deliverable definition

Consulting deliverables (reports, frameworks, recommendations) need to be named. "Strategic advice" is not a deliverable — a 20-page market analysis is.

When to ask a lawyer

Where AI ends, counsel begins.

BizDraft is a drafting tool. It is not a substitute for a lawyer who knows your specific situation. Reach for one when:

Licensed professions

Legal, medical, financial advisory, and accounting consulting have profession-specific liability rules. Use a lawyer familiar with your industry's standards of care.

High-stakes engagements

M&A advisory, restructuring work, or board-level consulting on major decisions warrant attorney involvement on both sides of the agreement.

Equity compensation

If any part of compensation involves options, warrants, or equity grants, you need a securities attorney to structure and document that correctly.

FAQ

Consulting agreement questions.

What is the difference between a consulting agreement and a service agreement?

Service agreements govern the delivery of defined outputs. Consulting agreements govern the delivery of expertise, judgment, and recommendations. Consulting agreements need additional clauses: advice limitations, conflict-of-interest disclosures, and scope-of-advice definitions that service agreements don't require.

Can a consultant work with competing clients?

Yes, unless the agreement prohibits it. Most consulting agreements allow work with other clients, including competitors, but require disclosure and information barriers. Exclusivity clauses exist but typically come at a premium.

What happens to unused retainer hours?

That depends on what you negotiate. Retainers are typically "use it or lose it" — unused hours don't roll over unless the agreement explicitly allows rollover. BizDraft lets you specify your preferred policy.

Who owns the frameworks and methodologies I develop for the client?

Pre-existing methodology and tools belong to the consultant. Work product created specifically for the client (reports, analyses, custom frameworks) can be assigned to the client on payment. The distinction should be explicit in the agreement.
