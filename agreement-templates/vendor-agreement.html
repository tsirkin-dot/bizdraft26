What a vendor agreement is for

Buy services with clear expectations and clear exits.

A vendor agreement governs the relationship between a company and an outside service provider — typically a technology vendor, SaaS platform, staffing firm, or professional services vendor. It defines service levels, data handling obligations, liability limits, and the conditions under which either party can exit the relationship.

Who uses it

Procurement teams, operations leaders, and small business owners evaluating and contracting with outside service vendors. Used by both the buying company and the vendor to establish baseline terms.

When to use it

Before onboarding any vendor who will access your data, integrate with your systems, or provide services your business depends on. Especially important for SaaS vendors where data portability and exit terms matter.

When not to use it

Not appropriate for commodity purchases without ongoing service — use a purchase order. When the vendor is providing software under a standard EULA, negotiate addendums to their standard terms rather than replacing them.

Common mistakes

Where vendor agreements leave you exposed.

The most common ways companies sign vendor contracts that are hard to exit or leave their data at risk.

Mistake

Auto-renewal without notice

Vendor contracts that auto-renew without adequate advance notice can lock you into another year before you realize the renewal window closed. Require 60–90 day advance notice and a clear opt-out process.

Mistake

No data export rights

Signing without data portability provisions means the vendor can hold your data hostage on termination. Require the right to export all your data in a usable format before and after termination.

Mistake

No breach notification SLA

When a vendor is breached, you may have regulatory obligations that require notifying affected individuals. Without a breach notification clause, you may not learn about a vendor breach in time to meet your own obligations.

Mistake

Unlimited price increase rights

Vendor contracts with "pricing subject to change at any time" give vendors unlimited leverage on renewal. Require advance notice and a cap on annual price increases.

When to ask a lawyer

Where AI ends, counsel begins.

BizDraft is a drafting tool. It is not a substitute for a lawyer who knows your specific situation. Reach for one when:

HIPAA or financial data

Vendors accessing protected health information or financial data require Business Associate Agreements (BAA) or specific regulatory addendums. Attorney review is required before signing.

Critical infrastructure vendors

Vendors providing services your business cannot operate without — core payment processing, primary cloud infrastructure — warrant detailed due diligence and attorney review of termination and transition terms.

International vendors

Vendors outside the US may be subject to GDPR, PIPEDA, or other data protection regimes that affect how they handle your data. Cross-border data transfer clauses require attorney review.

FAQ

Vendor agreement questions.

Should I use the vendor's standard contract or my own?

Vendor-drafted contracts favor the vendor. Using your own template shifts negotiating leverage. Even if you ultimately sign a modified version of the vendor's paper, having your own draft lets you identify the gaps between what you want and what the vendor is offering.

What is an SLA and why does it matter?

A service level agreement defines the minimum performance standards the vendor must meet — uptime, response time, resolution time. More importantly, it defines what happens when those standards are not met: service credits, termination rights, or other remedies. Without an SLA, "we'll do our best" is the only commitment.

What should happen to my data when I terminate the vendor?

The vendor should provide a data export in a standard, portable format (CSV, JSON, etc.) within a defined number of days after termination. After the transition period, the vendor should certify deletion of all your data. Both obligations should be explicit in the agreement.

Can I terminate a vendor contract early?

Only if the agreement allows it. Many vendor contracts have annual or multi-year minimums with early termination fees. If for-convenience termination is important to you, negotiate it before signing — a 60 or 90-day notice period for-convenience exit is a reasonable ask for most services.
