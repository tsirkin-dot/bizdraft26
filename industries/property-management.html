What makes property management contracts different

Every state has its own rules. A generic lease can be unenforceable — or worse, legally invalid.

Residential leases are one of the most state-regulated documents in U.S. law. Security deposit caps vary by state. Required disclosures differ (lead paint, mold, radon, bed bugs). Rent control rules apply in some jurisdictions. A generic template from another state can expose landlords to fines, disputes, and unenforceability.

Industry-specific clauses

State-specific security deposit cap. Required disclosures (lead paint, mold, radon, bed bugs — varies by state). Smoking and pet policy. Late rent grace period and fee. Lease renewal and month-to-month holdover terms. Maintenance responsibilities split.

Common deal types

Common deal types

Five shapes a landlord engagement takes.

Standard

12-month fixed-term lease

Standard annual residential lease with state-specific deposit cap, required disclosures, and renewal notice requirement. The most common residential tenancy structure.

Flexible

Month-to-month tenancy

No fixed term. Either party can terminate with 30-day notice. Higher monthly rate reflects the flexibility. State-compliant notice requirements built in.

STR

Short-term vacation rental

Airbnb-style or direct booking under 30 days. Nightly rate, damage deposit, house rules, early departure policy, and check-in/check-out terms.

Renewal

Lease renewal with rent adjustment

Renewing an existing tenancy at a new rate. Amendment to the original lease, updated rent, updated terms, and acknowledgment of continued disclosure requirements.

Corporate

Corporate housing lease

Tenant is a company (not an individual). Company as guarantor, furnished unit, utilities included, and shorter fixed term at a corporate rate.

The property management risk map

Five landlord risks. Most are preventable with a compliant lease.

Deposit cap violation

Collecting more than the state allows. Fix: BizDraft applies your state's deposit cap automatically — you enter the amount and the AI flags if it exceeds the legal limit.

Missing disclosures

Failing to include required state disclosures (lead paint, mold, bed bugs) can void your lease and expose you to fines. Fix: BizDraft includes the required disclosures for your state automatically.

Holdover ambiguity

Tenant stays past the lease end with no clear terms. Fix: explicit holdover clause converting to month-to-month at a specified rate, or requiring immediate vacate with defined remedies.

Maintenance dispute

Who's responsible for what repair? Fix: explicit maintenance responsibility section — landlord's obligations vs. tenant's obligations, with notification requirements.

Unauthorized occupants

Tenant moves in additional people not on the lease. Fix: occupancy limit and unauthorized occupant clause with defined remedies.
