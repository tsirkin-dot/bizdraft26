What makes home services contracts different

You're working on someone else's property. The contract has to protect you both.

Home services work involves access to private property, physical risk, and work that can't easily be undone. The contract has to address scope precisely (so you're not responsible for work you didn't quote), liability for property damage, and how changes to the original job are handled.

Industry-specific clauses

Property access authorization. Scope-of-work definition with change-order process. Liability for property damage. Insurance certificate requirements. Materials sourcing and cost. Permit responsibility. Callout fees for one-off visits.

Common deal types

One-time project, recurring seasonal service, recurring weekly or monthly service, emergency callout, and subcontractor agreements with general contractors.

The home services risk map

Five on-site risks. All preventable in the contract.

Property damage liability

You break something on-site. Fix: explicit liability clause defining your responsibility for direct damage caused by your work, and insurance certificate requirements.

Scope dispute

"I thought that was included." Fix: detailed scope-of-work definition in the agreement plus a change-order process for anything discovered or added mid-job.

Non-payment after completion

Job is done but the check never arrives. Fix: deposit upfront, progress payments, and final payment due before you leave the property on the last day.

Late cancellation

Client cancels the day of a scheduled crew visit. Fix: cancellation fee clause for notice received within 24-48 hours of the scheduled service.

Materials cost overrun

Material prices change between quote and job. Fix: materials cost clause — either fixed-price with cost risk on you, or cost-plus with client approval above a threshold.
