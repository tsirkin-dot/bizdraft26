What makes logistics contracts different

Logistics is about custody of goods. The contract has to define who owns the risk from pickup to delivery.

A logistics service agreement needs to define route scope (what you're hauling, where, how often), payment terms (per mile, per load, weekly), cargo liability (what you're responsible for if something goes wrong), equipment terms (who maintains what), and what happens when a pickup is missed or a load is late.

Industry-specific clauses

Route scope and service territory. Rate structure (per mile, per load, weekly flat). Cargo liability cap and insurance requirements. Equipment maintenance responsibility. Fuel surcharge terms. On-time delivery SLA. Exclusivity restrictions. Driver independent contractor vs. employee language.

Common deal types

Owner-operator carrier agreement, dedicated route contract, broker-carrier agreement, last-mile delivery, freight hauling subcontract, and local delivery route agreement.

The logistics risk map

Five logistics risks. All preventable in the contract.

Cargo loss or damage

Cargo is damaged in transit. Fix: cargo liability clause defining your responsibility limit, insurance requirements, and claim process. Liability capped at cargo value or a defined maximum.

Rate dispute

Client disputes the rate on an invoice. Fix: rate clause specifying per-mile, per-load, or flat weekly rate, plus fuel surcharge formula and any accessorial charges.

Route termination

Client eliminates a dedicated route with no notice. Fix: termination notice clause requiring 30–60 days' notice to terminate a dedicated route, with payment for committed expenses during the notice period.

IC vs. employee classification

State labor agency determines your owner-operator is actually an employee. Fix: IC classification language consistent with state law — but note this is complex and may require legal review in your state.

Equipment damage dispute

Client claims their trailer was damaged by your driver. Fix: equipment condition clause with pre- and post-use documentation and a defined damage reporting process.
