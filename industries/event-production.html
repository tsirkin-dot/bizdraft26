What makes event contracts different

Events are date-dependent, non-refundable by nature, and subject to forces outside anyone's control. The contract has to address all three.

An event contract is fundamentally different from an ongoing services agreement. The date is irreplaceable. The deposit is non-negotiable because you're turning away other bookings. And force majeure — weather, government restrictions, venue issues — needs to be defined precisely so everyone knows what happens when the event can't proceed.

Industry-specific clauses

Date lock and non-refundable deposit. Tiered cancellation fees by days-before-event. Force majeure definition (weather, government, venue damage, pandemic). Postponement vs. cancellation distinction. Overtime and over-capacity charges. Vendor coordination responsibility. Guest-artist and performer terms.

The event risk map

Five event risks. Most are preventable with the right contract.

Last-minute cancellation

Client cancels six weeks out. You've turned away other bookings, committed vendors, and spent hours on planning. Fix: tiered cancellation fee schedule — the closer to the event, the higher the percentage of the total fee retained.

Force majeure ambiguity

Unexpected event prevents the day from happening. Fix: explicit force majeure clause defining what qualifies (weather, government order, venue damage, pandemic), and whether it triggers postponement rights or a refund.

Scope expansion

"Can you also coordinate the rehearsal dinner and post-event brunch?" Fix: scope definition in the agreement plus a change-order process for events, services, or venues added after signing.

Vendor failure

A sub-vendor no-shows on the day. Client holds you responsible. Fix: vendor coordination clause defining your role as coordinator, not guarantor — you're responsible for selecting qualified vendors, not for their performance.

Overtime and overage

Event runs two hours over the planned end time. Fix: overtime rate clause defining the per-hour charge for time beyond the contracted event end, billed to client at a defined rate.
