What makes copywriting contracts different

Words are intellectual property. The contract has to say who owns them and where they can go.

A copywriting contract isn't just a services agreement. It has to address ownership of the written work, what the client is licensed to do with it, whether you retain the right to display it in your portfolio, and who's credited as the author (particularly in ghostwriting arrangements).

Industry-specific clauses

Content ownership and IP transfer on payment. Usage rights by channel and duration. Ghostwriting credit waiver or byline requirement. Revision rounds with change-order trigger. Publication rights and exclusivity windows.

Common deal types

Project-based copy, content retainer, ghostwriting, white-label content, SEO content programs, email sequences, and agency subcontracting. Each structure has different IP, credit, and payment needs.

Common deal types

Five shapes a copywriting engagement takes.

Project

Website copy or campaign

Fixed deliverable set: homepage, landing pages, email sequence. Copywriting services agreement with milestone payment and IP transfer on final delivery.

Ongoing

Monthly content program

Regular blog posts, newsletters, or social content. Content retainer with monthly quota, revision rounds, and IP transfer rolling with each delivery.

Ghostwriting

Books, articles & thought leadership

Client publishes under their name. Ghostwriting agreement with full IP assignment, credit waiver, and confidentiality of the arrangement.

White-label

Content for agencies

Writing delivered to an agency that sells it to their clients under their brand. Subcontractor agreement with non-solicitation and white-label rights.

SEO

Content program — SEO focus

Volume content delivery with defined keyword requirements. Monthly deliverable agreement with performance disclaimer — you deliver the content, not the ranking.

The copywriter risk map

Five risks copywriters face. All contractable.

Unpaid content use

Client publishes the website before paying the final invoice. Fix: IP transfer clause conditioned on receipt of full payment. Until paid, you own the copy.

Competitor exclusivity

Client assumes you won't write for their competitors — you assumed nothing was said. Fix: explicit exclusivity clause defining whether or not you're restricted, and for how long.

Revision spiral

"Just one more round of tweaks" — six rounds later. Fix: revision limit in the agreement with a defined rate for additional rounds beyond the included count.

Portfolio restriction

Client restricts you from showing the work in your portfolio. Fix: portfolio rights clause preserving your right to display work, subject to any NDA carve-outs.

Scope expansion

"Can you also do the email sequence and social posts?" Fix: clear scope definition and a change-order clause that prices and documents expansions before work begins.
