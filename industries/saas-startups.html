What makes SaaS contracts different

Early-stage SaaS has four critical legal moments. Most teams handle zero of them before something goes wrong.

SaaS startups need to establish who owns the IP (especially work done before formal formation), how co-founders split equity and responsibilities, what happens when someone leaves, and what the vendor and customer agreements look like. Each of these is a different document with different legal structure.

Industry-specific clauses

IP assignment from pre-formation work. Co-founder equity split with vesting schedule. Decision-making authority and dispute resolution. Developer IP ownership (work-for-hire vs. contractor). SaaS vendor terms (acceptable use, uptime, data handling). Mutual NDA for investor and partner pitches.

Common deal types

Co-founder partnership agreement, IP assignment from founding team, contractor/freelance developer agreement, vendor terms for SaaS tools, mutual NDA for fundraising pitches, and early customer pilot agreement.

The SaaS startup stack

The documents every early-stage SaaS team needs to have in place.

Generate early. The co-founder agreement you skip on day one is the lawsuit you're dealing with at Series A.

Founding

Co-founder agreement

Equity split, vesting schedule, roles and responsibilities, decision-making authority, IP contribution, and what happens when a co-founder leaves.

IP cleanup

IP assignment agreement

Assign intellectual property developed before formation to the company. Covers code, designs, domain names, and other pre-company work product.

Development

Contractor / developer agreement

Work-for-hire clause ensuring all code and deliverables belong to the company. IP assignment, confidentiality, and no-compete for the project scope.

Vendors

Vendor terms of service

Terms for customers using your SaaS product. Acceptable use policy, uptime SLA, data handling, limitation of liability, and termination rights.

Pitching

Mutual NDA

Two-way confidentiality for investor meetings, partner discussions, and strategic conversations where both sides share sensitive information.

Early customers

Pilot / beta agreement

Early customer pilot with defined scope, no-charge or discounted pricing, feedback obligations, confidentiality, and no-warranty disclaimer for pre-production software.

The SaaS startup risk map

Five early-stage risks. All preventable with the right document in place.

Co-founder departure

A co-founder leaves at month eight with 50% of the equity fully vested and no obligation to continue. Fix: co-founder agreement with vesting schedule, cliff, and buyback rights for unvested shares on departure.

Pre-formation IP

The MVP was built before the company was incorporated. The founder, not the company, technically owns it. Fix: IP assignment agreement signed at incorporation — transfers all pre-formation work to the entity.

Contractor IP ownership

A freelance developer builds a feature. Without a work-for-hire clause, they may retain ownership of the code. Fix: developer agreement with explicit IP assignment to the company for all work product.

Customer liability

A customer's business suffers a loss they attribute to a bug in your software. Fix: SaaS vendor terms with limitation of liability — your liability is capped at fees paid, and consequential damages are excluded.

Pitch confidentiality

Strategic partner shares your unreleased roadmap after a pitch. Fix: mutual NDA signed before any sensitive disclosure, covering both parties' confidential information.
