What makes light manufacturing contracts different

Fabricators deal with materials, production timelines, quality standards, and shared physical space. Generic service agreements cover none of that.

Light manufacturing contracts need to address both sides: the customer-facing side (what you're building, delivery timelines, quality acceptance, payment milestones) and the supply side (materials sourcing, vendor reliability, cost escalation). Add shared workshop or sublease arrangements and you've got a set of legal needs that generic templates completely miss.

Industry-specific clauses

Production specification and acceptance criteria. Material sourcing responsibility. Cost escalation clause for raw materials. Quality inspection rights. Delivery timeline with delay penalty or grace period. IP ownership of design files and tooling. Sublease terms for shared shop space with equipment use rights.

Common deal types

Custom fabrication order, contract manufacturing agreement, material supply agreement, production retainer, shared workshop sublease, and co-maker space agreement.

Common deal types

Five shapes a light manufacturing engagement takes.

Custom order

One-off custom fabrication

Single custom piece or prototype. Fabrication agreement with production spec, 50% deposit, final payment on delivery, and acceptance criteria for the completed piece.

Production run

Batch production order

Producing a defined quantity of a product. Contract manufacturing agreement with unit price, total volume, lead time, quality inspection, and milestone payments.

Recurring

Ongoing contract manufacturing

Regular production of a client brand's product. Monthly or per-batch pricing, volume commitment, lead time, quality standards, and 60-day termination notice.

Supply

Raw material supply deal

Regular delivery of materials (metals, composites, components). Supply agreement with pricing, delivery schedule, quality standards, and cost escalation clause.

Space share

Shared workshop or makerspace

Subleasing part of a fabrication shop. Sublease with equipment use rights, maintenance responsibilities, permitted materials, safety requirements, and access hours.

The light manufacturing risk map

Five fabrication risks. All preventable in the contract.

Quality dispute

Client rejects delivered goods claiming they don't meet spec. Fix: quality acceptance criteria defined in the agreement before production begins, with an inspection process and defined remedy for non-conforming goods.

Material cost escalation

Raw material prices spike between quote and production. Fix: material cost escalation clause — fixed price for a defined period, then cost-plus with client approval above a threshold percentage.

Mid-production cancellation

Client cancels the order after you've sourced materials and started production. Fix: cancellation clause for in-progress orders — client pays for materials purchased and work completed to date.

Design file ownership

Who owns the CAD files, tooling, or production drawings created for the job? Fix: IP clause specifying that client-provided designs remain client property; designs created by you for the client transfer on full payment.

Shared space liability

Another maker in your shared shop damages your equipment or is injured. Fix: sublease agreement with equipment use responsibility, liability allocation, and insurance requirements for each occupant.
