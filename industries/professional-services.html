What makes professional services contracts different

You're handling sensitive financial data. The contract needs to reflect that responsibility — and limit it appropriately.

Bookkeepers, accountants, and financial advisors work with the most sensitive business information that exists. Their contracts need to address confidentiality rigorously, define the scope of services with precision, and include liability limitations that reflect what an advisory engagement actually guarantees.

Industry-specific clauses

Scope of services definition (what you do and what you don't). Client data responsibility. Confidentiality of financial records. Liability cap tied to fees paid. Advice-is-not-a-guarantee disclaimer. Renewal and termination for recurring engagements.

Common deal types

Monthly bookkeeping retainer, annual tax preparation, ongoing CFO advisory, one-time financial review, payroll services, and audit support engagements.

The professional services risk map

Five risks financial professionals face. All addressable in the engagement letter.

Scope liability

Client assumes you're doing something you're not. Fix: precise scope-of-services definition specifying exactly what's included — and a statement of what's expressly excluded.

Bad-data liability

Client provides inaccurate records; you file based on what they gave you. Fix: client data responsibility clause — your liability is limited to work performed on data provided by the client.

Confidentiality breach

Financial data shared with the wrong person or stored insecurely. Fix: explicit confidentiality clause with data handling standards and sub-contractor coverage.

Advice reliance

Client makes a major financial decision relying solely on your advice and it goes wrong. Fix: advice-is-advisory disclaimer and liability cap tied to fees paid for the engagement.

Non-payment on recurring

Client stops paying the monthly retainer but continues accessing services. Fix: clear payment terms with suspension right — service pauses if payment is overdue by defined number of days.
