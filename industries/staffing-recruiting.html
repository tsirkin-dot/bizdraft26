What makes recruiting contracts different

Recruiters work on contingency. The fee only triggers on a successful hire — and the contract has to define exactly what that means.

A recruiting agreement has to nail down the fee basis (percentage of base salary or total comp), what triggers payment (offer acceptance vs. start date vs. X days employed), the replacement guarantee if the hire doesn't work out, and whether you have an exclusivity window to prevent the client from hiring candidates you introduced without paying your fee.

Industry-specific clauses

Placement fee as a percentage of base salary or total comp. Triggering event (offer accepted, start date, or 30/60/90 days employed). Replacement guarantee terms. Exclusivity window. Off-limits / candidate non-solicitation. Retained vs. contingency fee structure.

Common deal types

Contingency recruiting, retained search, temp-to-hire staffing, contract staffing, executive search, and multi-position agreements with volume fee schedules.

The recruiting risk map

Five recruiting risks. All preventable in the agreement.

End-run around the fee

Client hires your candidate directly after you introduced them but outside your agreement window. Fix: candidate introduction clause with a defined ownership period — if client hires within 12 months, your fee is due.

Fee triggering dispute

Client argues your fee isn't due because the candidate didn't start. Fix: explicit triggering event — offer letter signed, start date, or X days employed — defined unambiguously in the agreement.

Replacement guarantee abuse

Hire works out but client terminates them for reasons unrelated to performance and demands a replacement. Fix: replacement guarantee with defined eligibility — involuntary termination for performance only, not layoffs or role elimination.

Multi-recruiter competition

Client is working with three recruiters on the same role. You find a great candidate but can't control if they apply directly. Fix: exclusivity clause locking your firm as the exclusive recruiter for the specific role during the search window.

Candidate poaching

Client directly solicits candidates you introduced for other positions without engaging you. Fix: candidate non-solicitation clause covering all candidates introduced through you for a defined period.
