How it works

From draft to signed: step by step.

The full workflow from deal description to signed agreement.

Step 1

Draft in BizDraft

Go to app.bizdraft.com/editor. Describe your deal — the parties, the services, the payment terms, the governing state. BizDraft generates a tailored agreement. No account required.

Step 2

Review and download

Read through the generated agreement. Each clause is explained in plain language. Edit any clause that doesn't fit your deal. Download the Word file or PDF when you're ready to send for signature.

Step 3

Upload to SignNow

Go to signnow.com. Create a free account if you don't have one. Upload the agreement file. The free tier allows you to send documents for e-signature.

Step 4

Add signature fields

In SignNow's editor, drag and drop signature fields, date fields, and any other required fields onto the document. Assign each field to the appropriate signer.

Step 5

Send and track

Enter the other party's email address and send. They receive an email with a signing link. They sign from any device — no SignNow account required on their end. You receive a notification when they sign.

Step 6

Signed document stored

The fully signed document is stored in SignNow and available for download by both parties. The signing audit trail is attached — timestamps, IP addresses, and signer identity confirmation.

For higher-volume use — bulk sending, advanced workflow automation, team features — SignNow's paid plans add those capabilities.

What "legally binding" means for e-signatures

  • ESIGN Act (federal): Electronic signatures are legally valid for most contracts under federal law. The law has been in effect since 2000.
  • UETA (state): The Uniform Electronic Transactions Act, adopted by most states, provides the state-level equivalent.
  • Audit trail: SignNow captures a timestamp, IP address, and email confirmation for each signature. This creates a record of when and by whom the document was signed.
  • What e-signature doesn't cover: Certain documents require wet ink signatures — wills, real estate deeds in some states, court documents. For the vast majority of commercial service agreements, e-signatures are fully valid.
