The two tools in the workflow and what each one does.

BizDraft — generates the agreement

Describe the deal. BizDraft produces a complete agreement tailored to your situation — with standard protective clauses, state-appropriate language, and plain-English clause explanations. Download the PDF. This is the document you'll send for signature.

SignNow — collects the signatures

Upload the PDF to SignNow. Add signature fields. Send to each party by email. They sign from any device — no SignNow account required. You track completion in real time. The signed document is stored with a full audit trail.

Legally binding. Documented. Done.

SignNow e-signatures are legally binding under the federal ESIGN Act and UETA for commercial agreements. The signed document includes a timestamped audit trail — evidence that the document was sent, viewed, and signed by each party.

Click-by-click

Every step in the workflow.

No ambiguity about what to do next. The complete sequence from draft to signed document.

Step 1

Generate the agreement

Go to app.bizdraft.com/editor. Enter: the type of agreement, the parties' names, the services being provided, payment amount and terms, project timeline, governing state. Click Generate. BizDraft produces the complete agreement.

Step 2

Review the draft

Read through the generated agreement. Use the plain-language clause explanations if a clause is unclear. Edit any clause that doesn't fit your deal. The Word file is fully editable; the PDF is ready for immediate signing if you're satisfied with the draft as generated.

Step 3

Download the PDF

Click Download PDF. Save the file to your computer. This is the document you'll upload to SignNow in the next step. (If you edited the Word file first, save it as PDF before uploading.)

Step 4

Upload to SignNow

Go to signnow.com. Create a free account (takes about 30 seconds — just an email and password). Click "Upload Document" and select the PDF you just downloaded from BizDraft.

Step 5

Add signature fields

In SignNow's document editor, drag a "Signature" field to the signature line for each party. Add a "Date" field next to each signature block. Assign each field to the appropriate signer by entering their email address as the signer for that field.

Step 6

Send for signature

Click "Send for Signature." Enter each signer's email address. Add an optional message (e.g., "Please review and sign the attached service agreement"). Click Send. Each signer receives an email with a link to the document.

Step 7

Track signing status

In SignNow's dashboard, you can see which signers have opened the document, who has signed, and who still needs to complete. Send reminders directly from SignNow if a signer hasn't acted after a few days.

Step 8

Receive and store the signed document

Once all parties have signed, SignNow sends a notification. The fully executed document — with all signatures, dates, and the audit trail — is available in your SignNow dashboard. Download it for your records. Both parties receive a copy by email.

High-volume users, teams, or businesses needing advanced workflow automation will find the paid plans address those needs.

What the signer needs to do

  • No account required. The person you send the signing request to does not need a SignNow account. They receive an email, click the link, review the document in their browser, and click to sign.
  • Any device. The signing link works on desktop, tablet, or phone. No software to install.
  • Their signature is captured. They can draw, type, or upload an image of their signature. The signature is cryptographically tied to their email address and the signing event.
  • They get a copy. Once all parties have signed, each signer receives the executed document automatically.
