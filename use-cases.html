By situation

The trigger event, not the document name.

You usually don't wake up thinking "I need an IP assignment agreement." You wake up thinking "I hired a designer and the work needs to be mine." This is how to find the right agreement when you don't know what it's called yet.

Onboarding a new client

You closed the deal. The work starts Monday.

Before the kickoff: scope, payment, IP, revisions, termination. The most common reason small businesses draft an agreement at all.

Fits: Service agreement · Project agreement · SOW

Hiring a contractor or freelancer

A designer, developer, or specialist is starting work for you.

IRS classification matters — get the 1099 line right. IP transfer matters more — the deliverables need to be yours.

Fits: Independent contractor agreement · NDA · IP assignment

Sharing sensitive information

A pitch meeting, a vendor demo, an investor conversation.

You're about to show the thing you don't want copied. One-way or mutual, signed before the call.

Fits: NDA (mutual or one-way)

Leasing space

An apartment to rent out. An office to take. A studio to sublet.

State-aware lease — deposit caps, smoking and pet rules, lead-paint disclosures where they apply. Move-in ready paperwork.

Fits: Residential lease · Commercial lease · Sublease

Starting a partnership

You and a co-founder are about to put it on paper.

Ownership splits, decision rights, capital, what happens when someone leaves. Better to draft it before anything goes wrong.

Fits: Partnership agreement · IP assignment

Recurring engagement

Monthly bookkeeping. Quarterly marketing. Ongoing maintenance.

Fixed monthly fee, included hours, rollover and cancellation rules. The shape that fits ongoing work.

Fits: Retainer agreement · Service agreement (recurring)

Licensing or transferring IP

Someone wants to use your work — for a fee, in a scope, in a market.

License it without giving it away. Field of use, exclusivity, term, royalty structure. Or transfer it outright with a clean assignment.

Fits: Licensing agreement · IP assignment

Vendor or supplier

A SaaS vendor, a supplier, a fulfillment partner.

SLA, data terms, liability caps, exit ramps. The terms that protect you when something downstream breaks.
