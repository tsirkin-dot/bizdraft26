legally binding?

The short answer: yes, if it meets the standard requirements for a valid contract. The drafting method doesn't determine enforceability — the contract's substance does. Here's what actually matters.

What makes a contract enforceable.

The three requirements

What makes a contract valid — regardless of how it was drafted.

Contract law is not concerned with who drafted the document. It's concerned with whether the agreement meets these requirements.

Competent parties

Both parties must have legal capacity to contract — they must be of legal age and mentally competent. For businesses, the person signing must have authority to bind the entity. Neither requirement has anything to do with how the document was drafted.

Mutual agreement and consideration

There must be offer, acceptance, and consideration — something of value exchanged by both parties. In a service agreement, one party provides services; the other pays. The agreement must clearly reflect this exchange. BizDraft drafts contracts that include the necessary consideration structure.

Lawful purpose

The contract must not require either party to do something illegal. This is separate from whether the document uses correct legal language. A poorly drafted contract for a lawful purpose may be enforceable; a well-drafted contract for an unlawful purpose is void.

What the drafting method does and doesn't affect

The tool drafted it. You signed it. You own it.

No court cares whether a contract was drafted by a partner at a law firm, a paralegal, a $29 template, or an AI. What courts look at: what the parties agreed to, whether the agreement is clear on the material terms, and whether the standard requirements for contract formation are met.

What the drafting method does affect: the quality of the clause language, whether the right protective provisions are present, and whether ambiguous language creates disputes down the line. BizDraft is built to produce clear clause language with standard protective provisions. But you should read every clause — because you're the one who signed it, and that's what the law holds you to.

What can make a contract unenforceable

  • Ambiguous material terms. If the deliverables, payment terms, or scope of work are unclear enough that a court can't determine what was agreed, the contract may be partially or fully unenforceable. This is a drafting problem, not an AI problem specifically.
  • Unlawful provisions. Clauses that violate law (an unenforceable non-compete in California, an illegal penalty clause) are void — and in some cases can affect the broader contract.
  • Lack of signature or formation. If the parties never formally agreed to the document — no signatures, no exchange, no evidence of acceptance — there's no contract regardless of how well it was drafted.
  • Fraud or duress. A contract signed under fraud or coercion is voidable. This has nothing to do with the drafting tool.

Common questions

Enforceability, by the most-asked questions.

Does it matter that AI drafted the contract?

No. Contract enforceability depends on what the agreement says and whether it meets formation requirements — offer, acceptance, consideration, competent parties, lawful purpose. The drafting method is not a legal element. A signed AI-drafted agreement has the same legal standing as a signed attorney-drafted agreement.

Does BizDraft guarantee the agreement is enforceable?

No. BizDraft does not guarantee enforceability — and doesn't claim to. Enforceability depends on the specific terms, how a court in your jurisdiction interprets them, and whether the contract meets formation requirements. These are fact-specific determinations that no drafting tool can guarantee in advance.

Do I need to sign it in a specific way for it to be enforceable?

For most service agreements, a signed writing — including a digital signature or e-signature via a service like SignNow — is sufficient. Some contract types (real estate transfers, agreements over certain thresholds in some states) have specific formal requirements. For your specific agreement type, confirm the signature requirements apply to what you're signing.

What if a clause is unenforceable in my state?

Most commercial contracts include a severability clause — which means an unenforceable provision is severed from the agreement without voiding the whole contract. BizDraft includes a severability clause as standard. If a specific clause is unenforceable in your state, the rest of the agreement typically remains binding.

Can the other party claim the contract is invalid because AI drafted it?

No. There is no legal basis for voiding a contract because it was drafted by an AI tool rather than a human. Contract validity turns on formation requirements and the content of the agreement — not on who or what wrote it. A party who signed the agreement is bound by its terms.
