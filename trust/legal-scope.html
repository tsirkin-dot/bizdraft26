The three distinctions

Drafting vs. advice vs. representation.

Three different things that are often confused. BizDraft does one of them.

Drafting — what BizDraft does

Generating a written agreement based on the facts you provide. BizDraft produces a document that reflects standard commercial terms, protective clauses, and state-appropriate language for the deal you describe. This is drafting.

Legal advice — what BizDraft doesn't do

Advising you on whether a specific clause is favorable given your negotiating position, your risk tolerance, and the other party's intentions. Predicting how a court will rule. Telling you whether to sign. These are legal judgments that require a licensed attorney.

Representation — not in scope

Negotiating on your behalf, appearing in proceedings, providing court-admissible legal opinions. BizDraft is a software product. It doesn't appear in court, doesn't negotiate, and doesn't represent any party.

Why this matters

The line is real — and it's there to protect you.

Legal advice can only be given by a licensed attorney. Software tools that claim to provide legal advice are making a claim they can't legally support. BizDraft doesn't make that claim. It drafts agreements. That's a narrower scope, honestly stated.

The practical implication: BizDraft can generate a solid first draft with standard protective clauses for most service agreements. It cannot tell you whether the deal is a good idea, whether you should accept the counterparty's terms, or what your rights are if the contract is breached. Those are questions for an attorney.

What BizDraft won't claim

  • "Lawyer-quality" output — because lawyer quality requires legal judgment, not just legal language.
  • "Guaranteed enforceable" — because enforceability depends on how a court interprets specific clauses in your specific state, not on the drafting tool.
  • "No attorney needed" — because some situations genuinely require an attorney, and we won't tell you otherwise.
  • "100% accurate" — because AI models can make errors, and you should read every clause before signing.

What BizDraft is built to do well

  • Generate a tailored first draft from your deal description — faster and more specific than a generic template.
  • Include standard protective clauses (IP assignment, liability cap, termination, confidentiality) that people often don't know to include.
  • Explain each clause in plain language so you know what you're signing.
  • Export a Word file that an attorney can review and mark up — reducing their time on your legal bill.

Common questions

Legal scope, by the most-asked questions.

Is BizDraft a law firm?

No. BizDraft is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. Using BizDraft does not create an attorney-client relationship. BizDraft is a software product that generates draft agreements.

Does using BizDraft mean I don't need a lawyer?

Not always. For high-value deals, regulated industries, or active negotiation with a sophisticated counterparty, an attorney's judgment is not replaceable. BizDraft is a starting point, not a substitute for legal counsel in high-stakes situations.

Can I give my BizDraft output to an attorney to review?

Yes, and we encourage it for high-stakes agreements. Download the Word file, send it to your attorney with "please review and mark up." They'll review a draft faster than drafting from scratch, which saves time on your legal bill.

Is the output "legal" in the sense that it uses correct legal language?

BizDraft produces agreements in standard commercial legal language, including standard clause structures. Whether any specific clause is correct for your specific situation is a judgment call that depends on your state, your deal, and your attorney's review.
