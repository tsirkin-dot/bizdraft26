The agency stack

Four documents every agency needs on file.

Generate them once. Reuse across every client relationship.

Master contract

Master service agreement (MSA)

The umbrella contract signed once per client. Governs all future SOWs: liability, IP, termination, governing law.

Per-project

Statement of work (SOW)

Scope, deliverables, timeline, payment for a specific engagement. Issued under the MSA — no need to re-sign the framework.

Recurring revenue

Retainer agreement

Fixed monthly fee, included hours, rollover rules, and notice period for cancellation. The document that locks in monthly revenue.

Contractor management

Subcontractor agreement

Flow-down IP, confidentiality, and scope obligations to every freelancer or studio you bring in on a client project.

Before the pitch

NDA

Mutual confidentiality for strategy discussions, creative briefs, and any sensitive client data shared before the MSA is signed.

Common deal types

Five shapes an agency engagement takes.

Project-based

One-off campaign or build

MSA + SOW. Fixed scope, fixed price, IP transfers on final payment. Clean close.

Ongoing relationship

Monthly retainer client

MSA signed once, retainer agreement covers the ongoing relationship. SOWs for any out-of-scope work.

White-label work

You are the subcontractor

Another agency hires you to deliver. Subcontractor agreement with IP flow-down and confidentiality from the prime's client.

Studio build

You hire freelancers for a project

You are the prime. Subcontractor agreement with your freelancers. Flow-down IP. You own what your subs produce.

Pitch phase

Strategy-first, contract second

NDA before the brief. MSA after the pitch. Protects your ideas if they don't hire you.

Enterprise client

Big-co procurement requirements

MSA structured for enterprise review. Insurance requirements, SLA clauses, indemnification limits. Generate a baseline; have counsel review.

The agency risk map

Five risks. All preventable in the contract.

Scope bleed

Retainer hours consumed by out-of-scope requests. Fix: SOW ceiling + change-order clause on every engagement.

IP leakage to subs

Subcontractor owns work they created. Fix: subcontractor agreement with IP assignment to agency on delivery.

Retainer cancellation risk

Client walks with zero notice after you've staffed for them. Fix: 30-day notice + early-termination fee clause.

Client poaching subs

Client hires your contractor directly. Fix: non-solicitation clause in the MSA.

Payment lag

30-day invoices becoming 90-day realities. Fix: net-terms clause, late-fee rate, and work-suspension rights.
