The jobs to be done

Five legal complexities remote teams face that local businesses don't.

Working across states and countries creates exposure that a single-state business never encounters. Each one has a clause that addresses it.

Contractor across state lines

IC classification standards differ by state. California ABC test, Massachusetts IC test — the state where the contractor works governs. IC agreement with jurisdiction awareness.

Non-compete enforceability

Non-competes are unenforceable in California, Minnesota, and a growing list of states. The clause needs to acknowledge the contractor's state law or it's worthless.

Cross-border IP assignment

Contractors in Canada, the EU, or elsewhere may have statutory rights to their work. IP assignment language needs to cover international scenarios.

Governing law for a distributed team

Company in Delaware, founder in Texas, contractor in Germany. Which law governs? Governing-law clause with jurisdiction selection makes the answer explicit.

Remote worker classification

A contractor who works exclusively for you, full-time, remotely looks like an employee in many states. IC agreement with proper independence language matters more, not less, at distance.

The remote-team risk map

Five distributed-team risks. All addressable in the contract.

Unenforceable non-compete

California, Minnesota, and others make non-competes unenforceable. Fix: state-aware IC agreement with non-solicitation substitution.

Misclassification in strict states

California ABC test is stricter than federal standard. Fix: IC agreement with California-specific independence language if the contractor is in CA.

International IP gap

EU and Canadian statutory moral rights may limit IP assignment. Fix: IP assignment that explicitly waives moral rights where legally permitted.

Ambiguous governing law

Contract dispute in a multi-state team — which court? Fix: explicit governing-law clause in every agreement.

Remote employee nexus

Hiring a W-2 employee in a new state creates tax and labor law obligations in that state. Fix: employment agreement referencing the employee's state. Consult a tax advisor on nexus obligations.
