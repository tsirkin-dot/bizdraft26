Common deal types

Five shapes a freelancer engagement takes.

One-time

Single project, fixed price

A logo, a website, a short campaign. Service agreement + SOW, IP transfer on final payment.

Recurring

Monthly retainer

Ongoing content, ongoing maintenance, ongoing advisory. Retainer agreement with rollover and notice.

Hourly

Time-and-materials

Open-ended work, hourly rate, monthly invoicing. Service agreement with hours-based payment terms.

Equity / barter

Equity-for-work, swap, partnership

When the deal isn't straight cash. Service agreement with non-standard payment terms — review with counsel.

Partnership

Co-creation, joint venture

Two freelancers building something together. Partnership agreement, IP joint ownership, profit splits.

Sub-hire

You bring in another freelancer

Subcontractor agreement with flow-down terms, IP, and confidentiality.

The freelancer risk map

Five risks. All preventable in the contract.

Payment risk

Late, partial, or never. Fix: deposit, milestones, late-fee, suspension rights. See more →

Ownership risk

Client uses work before paying. Fix: IP transfer triggered by full payment.

Timeline risk

Project drags because client doesn't respond. Fix: approval-timeout clause.

Scope risk

"Can you just add one more thing?" Fix: change-order clause + revision limits.

Termination risk

Client walks at 80%. Fix: kill-fee clause for pre-termination work.
