The three promises

What we do. What we don't. Where the line is.

Privacy, AI honesty, legal scope. Everything else flows from these three.

Privacy by default

Your draft lives in your browser tab. We don't store it on our side, we don't share it, we don't sell it. There's nothing to sell.

Honest about the AI

BizDraft drafts with AI. We don't claim "lawyer-quality" or "guaranteed enforceable." Review every clause. Hand high-stakes drafts to an attorney before signing.

Not a law firm

BizDraft is a drafting tool. It doesn't provide legal advice and doesn't create an attorney–client relationship. For high-stakes situations, consult a lawyer.

What we store

As little as we can get away with.

The agreement you draft is built and edited in your browser. The text of your draft does not get saved to our database, does not get used to train future models, and does not get shared with anyone.

What we do collect, like any web service: standard server logs (IP, browser, page) and basic analytics for product improvement. We use those to keep the service running and to know which agreement types people actually use. We don't tie them to the contents of your draft, because the contents aren't on our side.

When you close the editor tab, your draft is gone — from our side. The downloaded file is yours, on your machine, your responsibility.

What this means in practice

  • No "your previous drafts" tab to come back to. The cost of privacy is that the file isn't there next time.
  • Download as Word or PDF before you close the tab. We'll prompt you.
  • Want to keep working on a draft tomorrow? Save the Word file and reopen it locally.

Where the data lives

  • The drafting model runs server-side. The text you type is sent to it the same way any web request is sent to any web service.
  • Once your session ends, those request logs follow standard short-retention rules — not indefinite storage.
  • Read the full privacy notice for the legal version.

What the AI does

A drafting engine, not a legal opinion.

BizDraft uses AI to draft your agreement — the clauses, the structure, the state-specific adjustments. The AI is doing what a fresh paralegal might do at 2 a.m.: assembling a competent first draft from the facts you provided.

What it isn't doing: making strategic legal judgments about whether this is the right kind of agreement for your business, whether the terms favor you in negotiation, or whether you should sign. Those are decisions only you and (where it matters) your attorney can make.

That's why we won't claim the output is "lawyer-quality," "guaranteed enforceable," or "100% accurate." It's a tailored draft. Read it. Edit it. Sign it when you're satisfied.

What you should always do

  • Read every clause before signing. Plain English helps, but the words still mean something.
  • For high-stakes agreements — significant money, regulated industries, anything with long-term enforceability — get a lawyer to review the Word file.
  • Treat the redline insertions like a colleague's suggestions. Accept what fits, adjust what doesn't, discard what doesn't apply.

What BizDraft will not do

  • Tell you whether to sign.
  • Predict how a court will rule on a clause.
  • Cover personal legal matters — divorce, wills, immigration. Those sit outside BizDraft's scope on purpose.
  • Replace an attorney for a high-stakes deal.

Common questions

Trust, by the most-asked questions.

Does BizDraft store my draft?

The agreement you draft lives in your browser tab, not on our server. Close the tab and it's gone on our side. We prompt you to download before you go.

Is an AI-drafted agreement legally binding?

A signed agreement is binding under the same rules as any other — both parties competent, clear terms, lawful purpose, signatures. The drafting tool doesn't change that.

What we won't claim is that any agreement is right for any deal. For high-stakes engagements, hand the Word file to your attorney before you sign.

How does the AI know my state's law?

BizDraft is built around the federal layer plus the state law of each U.S. state — security-deposit caps, contractor licensing rules, IP defaults, mandatory disclosures. When you pick a governing state in the intake, the draft is adjusted accordingly.

This is not a substitute for an attorney who knows the specifics of your situation. It's a substitute for not having a draft at all.

What if I want a lawyer to review it?

Recommended for high-stakes drafts. Download the Word file, send it to your attorney, let them mark it up. We don't lock the file or require you to come back to BizDraft.

Is BizDraft a law firm?

No. BizDraft is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. Using BizDraft does not create an attorney–client relationship.

Will my draft be used to train AI models?

No. The contents of your draft are not used to train the underlying models. Full details live in the privacy notice.
