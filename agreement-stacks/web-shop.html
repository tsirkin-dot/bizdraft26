What's in this stack

Five documents. One per phase of a web project.

Web design projects have a design phase, a build phase, an IP transfer moment, and an ongoing maintenance relationship. The stack covers each one.

Document 1

Website design agreement

The main contract for a website design or redesign engagement. Design scope, revisions, approval process, payment milestones, IP ownership, and launch conditions.

Document 2

Statement of work (SOW)

Per-project scope for the build phase. Pages, features, integrations, timeline, and acceptance criteria. Issued under or alongside the design agreement.

Document 3

IP transfer / assignment

Transfers ownership of the final design and codebase to the client on full payment. Until then, the work is yours. Separates deliverables from your background design tools and frameworks.

Document 4

Maintenance retainer

Monthly retainer for ongoing site maintenance, updates, and support. Included hours, response time, and cancellation notice. Converts a one-time project into recurring revenue.

Document 5

Subcontractor agreement

For developers, copywriters, or SEO specialists you bring in on a project. Flow-down IP assignment and confidentiality. You own what your subs deliver.

Why this stack

Three reasons web design businesses need the full stack.

IP transfer is the moment that matters most

Clients go live on sites they don't technically own yet if the IP transfer isn't tied to full payment. The IP assignment clause creates the explicit trigger.

Maintenance retainers are recurring revenue

The maintenance retainer converts every completed project into a monthly income stream. The contract makes the renewal automatic rather than requiring a new sales conversation.

Subcontractors must assign IP

Every developer or designer you bring in creates IP. Without a subcontractor agreement with IP assignment, you can't fully assign the final product to the client. The chain must be complete.

When to generate each document

Four ordered steps. Proposal to paid retainer.

1

When the project is agreed

Website design agreement + SOW. Scope, payment milestones, revision rounds, and IP transfer trigger. Signed before work begins.

2

Before every subcontractor

Subcontractor agreement with IP assignment before any freelance developer or designer starts work on the project.

3

At final payment

IP transfer/assignment document executed when full payment clears. Confirms formal handover of the design and codebase.

4

After launch

Propose the maintenance retainer. Generate, send, and convert the completed project into recurring monthly revenue.
