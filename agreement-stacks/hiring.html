What's in this stack

Four documents. Every contractor relationship covered.

Contractor relationships have four distinct exposure points: classification, confidentiality, IP ownership, and scope boundaries. The stack addresses each one with its own document.

Document 1

Independent contractor agreement

IC classification language, project scope, payment terms, independence requirements, and termination. The primary document that establishes the contractor relationship correctly.

Document 2

NDA

Confidentiality for every contractor who accesses business information, customer data, code, or unreleased products. Signed before onboarding starts.

Document 3

IP assignment

Assigns all work product created during the engagement to the company. Without this, the contractor may own what they built. Signed before any work begins.

Document 4

Scope of work

Specific deliverables, timeline, and acceptance criteria for the engagement. Scope definition is also a classification tool — specific scope supports IC status.

Why this stack

Three reasons the hiring stack is more than just an IC agreement.

Classification is in the details

IRS and state labor agencies look at behavioral control, financial control, and relationship type. The IC agreement combined with a specific SOW creates the documented evidence of independence.

IP ownership is the silent gap

Work-for-hire doctrine applies to employees, not always to contractors. Without an explicit IP assignment, the contractor may retain ownership of everything they build. This gap is invisible until someone leaves.

Scope supports classification

A contractor with a specific, time-bounded SOW looks like an IC. A contractor with an open-ended "show up every day" arrangement looks like an employee. The SOW shapes both the scope and the classification argument.

When to generate each document

Four ordered steps. Contractor agreed to fully onboarded.

1

NDA before sharing sensitive business information with a prospective contractor.

2

IC agreement with IP assignment. Signed before any work begins. The IC agreement and IP assignment can be combined in one document or kept separate.

4

Store the signed IC agreement, NDA, IP assignment, and SOW. The documentation set you need if the classification is ever audited.
