What's in this stack

Four documents. One per type of brand relationship.

Brand partnerships have multiple exposure points: campaign scope, content rights, confidentiality, and production relationships. Each one has its own document.

Document 1

Influencer / brand-deal agreement

Campaign scope, deliverables, platforms, posting schedule, approval process, FTC disclosure requirements, payment terms, and kill-fee clause.

Document 2

Content licensing agreement

Grants the brand specific usage rights for content you create. Defines channels, duration, paid vs. organic amplification rights, and exclusivity window.

Document 3

NDA

Mutual confidentiality for brands sharing unreleased product details or campaign strategy before the deal is finalized. Signed before the creative brief.

Document 4

Service agreement for shoots

For editors, videographers, photographers, or co-creators working on brand content. IP assignment so the final product belongs to you, not the production team.

Why this stack

Three reasons creator businesses need more than an email agreement.

Usage rights define the real value

A brand running your content as paid ads is worth more than a single organic post. The licensing agreement separates organic rights from paid amplification and prices them separately.

Kill fees protect produced content

Brands cancel after production has started. Without a kill-fee clause tied to production stage, you absorb the cost. The influencer agreement includes the kill-fee trigger by default.

Production IP flows upward

Your editor or videographer creates IP. Without a service agreement with IP assignment, you can't fully own the content you're licensing to the brand. The stack closes the chain.

When to generate each document

Four ordered steps. Brief to posted content.

1

Before the brief

NDA if the brand shares unreleased product details. Before they tell you what they're launching.

2

When the deal is agreed

Influencer agreement with deliverables, platforms, FTC clause, payment, and kill-fee. Signed before production starts.

3

Before production team

Service agreement with IP assignment for every editor, videographer, or photographer. Signed before shoot day.

4

With the deliverable

Content licensing agreement if the brand wants to repurpose your content. Channels, duration, and paid amplification rights defined before anything goes live.
