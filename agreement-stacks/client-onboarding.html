What's in this stack

Four documents. The complete client onboarding package.

Client onboarding has four stages: confidentiality before the brief, main contract before work, scope document for clarity, and retainer for ongoing relationships. Each has its own document.

Document 1

Service agreement

The main engagement contract. Scope, payment, IP, liability, revision process, and termination. The document that governs the relationship from kickoff to close.

Document 2

Statement of work (SOW)

Specific deliverables, milestones, timeline, and acceptance criteria for the engagement. Issued with or alongside the service agreement. The document that defines exactly what "done" means.

Document 3

NDA

Mutual confidentiality signed before the client shares their strategy, data, or unreleased plans with you — and before you share your methods and pricing with them.

Document 4 (if recurring)

Retainer agreement

For ongoing engagements: monthly fee, included services, rollover, and cancellation notice. The document that converts a one-time project client into a recurring revenue relationship.

Why this stack

Three reasons the onboarding stack is better than just sending the contract.

Scope disputes start before kickoff

Client expectations form during the sales conversation, not during delivery. The SOW captures what the client thinks they're getting before work begins — closing the gap that creates disputes at the end.

NDA before the brief protects both sides

Client strategy shared without a signed NDA is unprotected. Your pricing and methods shared without one are too. The NDA makes the onboarding conversation safe for both parties.

Retainer at onboarding converts projects to recurring

Proposing the retainer at the start of the engagement — not six months later — makes it part of the relationship structure. The onboarding stack includes it by design.

When to generate each document

Four ordered steps. Signed deal to clean kickoff.

1

Before the discovery or briefing call

NDA if the client will share sensitive information or you'll share methods and pricing. Before the conversation happens.

2

When the deal is agreed

Service agreement with payment, IP, and termination terms. Signed before any work begins.

3

With the service agreement

SOW with specific deliverables, timeline, and acceptance criteria. Sent together with the service agreement as the complete onboarding package.

4

If the work is recurring

Retainer agreement if the engagement will be ongoing. Monthly fee, included services, and cancellation terms. Propose at onboarding, not after the project is complete.
