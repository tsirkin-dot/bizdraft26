What's in this stack

Five documents. One per stakeholder relationship.

SaaS businesses have four distinct contract relationships: customers, vendors, employees/contractors, and enterprise clients. The stack addresses each one.

Document 1

Terms of service

Customer-facing agreement governing access to the product. Acceptable use, payment terms, refund policy, data handling, limitation of liability, and IP ownership.

Document 2

Vendor agreements

For infrastructure, tooling, and service vendors. Data access rights, SLAs, confidentiality, and liability for any vendor who touches customer data.

Document 3

IC agreement for contractors

For developers, designers, and ops contractors. IC classification language, IP assignment, confidentiality, and scope. Covers every external person building the product.

Document 4

Employment agreement for first hires

For W-2 employees: role, compensation, confidentiality, IP assignment, and non-solicitation. Signed before day one of every hire.

Document 5

MSA for enterprise customers

Master service agreement for enterprise and mid-market clients. SLAs, data processing, indemnification, liability caps, and security requirements. Issued with an order form or SOW.

Why this stack

Three reasons SaaS businesses need a stack, not just a TOS.

Each relationship has different risk

Customer liability is limited by TOS. Vendor risk is governed by SLAs. Employee IP chain is covered by employment agreements. One document for all of them creates gaps in every direction.

Enterprise deals need an MSA

Self-serve TOS won't close an enterprise customer. An MSA with negotiable SLA and security terms is what procurement teams need. Generate the baseline; your lawyer reviews the red-lines.

The IP chain must be airtight

Every contractor and employee who touches the codebase needs an IP assignment. The chain is reviewed at Series A and at acquisition. Close it before growth makes it complicated.

When to generate each document

Four ordered steps. Launch to enterprise-ready.

1

Before launch

Terms of service in place before the first customer signs up. Governs every self-serve account from day one.

2

Before every contractor and hire

IC agreement or employment agreement with IP assignment. Closes the chain before anyone touches the product.

3

Before every vendor

Vendor agreement for any tool or service with access to customer data. Data handling and liability included.

4

When the first enterprise deal starts

MSA baseline generated for enterprise procurement. Have counsel review any customer red-lines before countersigning.
