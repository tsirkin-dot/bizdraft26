What's in this stack

Five documents. One per risk category.

Each document in the stack handles a distinct part of the client relationship. Together, they close the gaps a handshake leaves open.

Document 1

Service agreement

The master contract for every client. Scope, payment, IP, liability, and termination. Thirteen sections covering the full engagement lifecycle.

Document 2

NDA

Mutual confidentiality before the engagement begins. Covers strategy, financials, and client data shared during scoping conversations.

Document 3

Independent contractor agreement

For when a client wants to formalize your IC status. Covers classification, scope, deliverables, payment, and independence language.

Document 4

IP assignment

Transfers ownership of work product to the client upon full payment. Until payment clears, the work is yours. Can be standalone or embedded in the service agreement.

Document 5

Invoice terms

Net payment terms, late-payment fee, deposit requirements, and work-suspension rights. The payment terms that make invoices get paid.

Why this stack

Three reasons the solo creator stack covers what a single contract doesn't.

Each document handles a different moment

NDA before the brief. Service agreement before work starts. IP assignment on payment. Invoice terms with every invoice. The stack covers the full lifecycle.

One document can't do everything

A service agreement that tries to be an NDA, IC agreement, and IP assignment at once is too long to read and too generic to enforce. Each document stays focused on its purpose.

Reuse across every client

Generate once, adjust rate and scope per engagement. The stack becomes your standard operating procedure rather than a custom effort each time.

When to generate each document

Four ordered steps. New client to protected engagement.

1

Before the discovery call

Generate and send the NDA if the client will share sensitive strategy, financials, or unreleased products during scoping.

2

When the deal is agreed

Generate the service agreement with scope, rate, and IP terms. Send before any work begins — same day as the verbal agreement.

3

When the client requests IC classification

Generate the IC agreement if the client needs to formalize your contractor status for their records or tax filings.

4

With every invoice

Include the invoice terms on every bill. Net terms, late fee, and IP transfer trigger on final payment are stated explicitly.
