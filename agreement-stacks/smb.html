What's in this stack

Five documents. One per business relationship.

Every small business has the same five relationships: clients, employees, contractors, vendors, and confidential relationships. Each one needs its own document.

Document 1

Service agreement

Scope, payment, liability, IP, and termination for every client engagement. The contract that turns a verbal agreement into a binding one.

Document 2

Employment agreement

For W-2 employees: role, compensation, schedule, confidentiality, IP assignment, and termination. Signed before the first day.

Document 3

Vendor agreement

For every regular supplier: delivery obligations, pricing, payment terms, liability limits, and remedy for non-delivery.

Document 4

Subcontractor agreement

For contractors delivering part of a client engagement. Flow-down IP and confidentiality from the prime contract. You remain responsible to the client.

Document 5

NDA

For employees, contractors, and partners who access pricing, customer lists, operational processes, or financial data. The confidentiality document for every sensitive relationship.

Why this stack

Three reasons every SMB needs these five on file before the problem arrives.

The gaps surface at the worst moment

Client disputes, contractor IP claims, and vendor non-delivery all show up after the relationship is already in trouble. The time to close each gap is before it opens.

Five documents, every business relationship covered

Client, employee, contractor, vendor, confidential partner — five relationships, five documents. The stack doesn't require a lawyer for routine relationships, only for the complex ones.

Generate once, reuse for every instance

Each document is a template for a category of relationship. New client, same service agreement structure. New employee, same employment agreement structure. Generate, adapt, send.

When to generate each document

Four ordered steps. Business open to fully papered operation.

1

First client

Service agreement before any work starts. Scope, payment, and termination. The document that makes the deal real.

2

First hire or contractor

Employment agreement or IC agreement + NDA signed before day one. Role, compensation, and confidentiality defined before any work begins.

3

First vendor

Vendor agreement before the first purchase order. Delivery obligations, payment terms, and remedy clause.

4

First subcontracted delivery

Subcontractor agreement with IP flow-down before any contractor touches client work.
