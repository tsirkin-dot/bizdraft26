The key dimensions

Cost, experience, and output quality.

The day-one experience is the biggest difference. Here's what each one actually feels like.

Cost

BizDraft: Free. No account.

LawDepot: Free 7-day trial, then $33/month (or $22/month annual). After the trial, download requires a subscription. Some documents available as one-time purchases.

Day-one experience

BizDraft: Describe your deal in plain English. The AI reads it and generates a draft. No questionnaire, no account, no form to fill out.

LawDepot: Choose a document type, answer a multi-step questionnaire, see a template populate. You fill in the blanks; the structure is fixed.

State awareness

BizDraft: You specify your governing state; the AI adjusts relevant clauses (IP defaults, payment rules, mandatory disclosures) accordingly.

LawDepot: Questionnaire includes a state selector that populates state-specific language. Both handle state awareness; approaches differ.

When to use which

Questionnaire templates vs. AI generation.

The questionnaire model and the AI model produce different outputs for different situations.

BizDraft wins

Your deal doesn't fit the questionnaire

LawDepot's questionnaire is built for standard deal structures. When your terms are non-standard — unusual payment structure, complex IP provisions, tiered pricing — the questionnaire can't capture it. BizDraft's AI reads a description and builds accordingly.

BizDraft wins

You want plain-language output

BizDraft generates contracts in plain, readable language with explanatory comments. LawDepot's templates use standard legal language — functional but less accessible for business owners without legal backgrounds.

BizDraft wins

You want no account and no friction

LawDepot requires an account to download. BizDraft generates and lets you download without any account or email. Zero friction from description to file.

LawDepot wins

You prefer guided step-by-step input

Some people prefer being asked questions rather than describing a deal in free text. LawDepot's questionnaire ensures you don't miss standard fields — it prompts every required piece of information.

LawDepot wins

You want a broad document library

LawDepot offers hundreds of document types across many legal categories. For businesses that need a wide variety of legal forms — not just service agreements — the breadth may be worth the subscription.

LawDepot wins

You need to store and retrieve documents

LawDepot stores your completed documents in your account. BizDraft generates locally — once you close the tab, the document is gone from our side. You keep what you download.

Honest verdict

Which one should you actually use?

If you know what you want and want to describe it in plain language, BizDraft will produce a more tailored result faster and for free. If you prefer a structured questionnaire that walks you through every field, LawDepot's approach may feel more comfortable — but it comes with a subscription cost and a fixed template structure.

For straightforward agreements where the questionnaire covers the deal adequately, either works. For non-standard deals, BizDraft's AI approach produces better output.

The honest summary

  • Cost: BizDraft is free. LawDepot is $33/month after the trial.
  • Account required: LawDepot yes. BizDraft no.
  • Document storage: LawDepot stores. BizDraft generates locally.
