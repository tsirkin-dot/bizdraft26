Drafting tool vs freelancer suite.

The key dimensions

Cost, bundling, and what you're actually getting.

Bonsai is a platform. BizDraft is a drafting tool. Different tools, different tradeoffs.

Cost

BizDraft: Free. No account, no trial.

Bonsai: Starts at $21/month (Starter), $32/month (Professional), $66/month (Business). Annual plans reduce the monthly rate. No meaningful free tier for contract creation.

What's bundled

BizDraft: Agreement drafting only. One thing, done well.

Bonsai: Contracts + proposals + invoicing + time tracking + task management + CRM + expense tracking. An all-in-one freelancer business platform.

Agreement drafting quality

BizDraft: AI-tailored to your specific deal. Not a template — a draft built from your description.

Bonsai: Templates with variable fields. Designed for typical freelance engagements. Less flexibility for non-standard terms or industry-specific clauses.

When to use which

The contract is only part of the picture.

The question is whether you need the full suite or just the agreement.

BizDraft wins

You just need the contract

You already have invoicing software. You already have a CRM. You just need a well-drafted service agreement for the project that starts next week. BizDraft delivers that for free without subscribing to a platform you don't need.

BizDraft wins

Your deal has non-standard terms

Bonsai's templates are built for the typical freelance engagement. Unusual IP clauses, complex payment structures, or industry-specific provisions often can't be expressed in a template's variable fields.

BizDraft wins

You want zero commitment

No monthly commitment, no account to cancel, no data to delete. One draft, download, done. BizDraft has no ongoing relationship to manage.

Bonsai wins

You want everything in one place

If you want your contracts, proposals, invoices, time logs, and client records all in one dashboard, Bonsai is built for exactly that. The bundling is the product.

Bonsai wins

You want integrated e-signatures and invoicing

Bonsai handles the contract-to-payment workflow end to end: draft, e-sign, invoice, track payment. BizDraft generates the draft; you use other tools for the rest (SignNow for signatures, etc.).

Bonsai wins

You're a full-time freelancer building a practice

If contracts are 10% of your operational need and you also need invoicing, project management, and client tracking, the Bonsai bundle may be cost-effective as a single tool replacement.

Honest verdict

Which one should you actually use?

If you need a single agreement — right now, tailored to a specific deal — BizDraft is faster, cheaper (free), and requires no commitment. The draft will be more tailored than Bonsai's template-fill output.

If you're building a freelance practice and want your business administration in one place — proposals, contracts, invoicing, time tracking — Bonsai's bundled value may justify the monthly fee. The trade-off is subscription cost and template flexibility.
