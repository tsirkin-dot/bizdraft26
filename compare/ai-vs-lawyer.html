The honest framing

Neither replaces the other. They do different things.

The question isn't AI or lawyer. It's which job each one is right for.

What AI does well

Generates a competent first draft quickly and for free. Includes standard protective clauses (IP, liability cap, termination, confidentiality) that many people don't know to include. Creates a starting point that covers the basics — so you're not negotiating from nothing.

What a lawyer does well

Makes strategic legal judgments. Advises on risk allocation. Negotiates on your behalf. Knows your state's case law, not just the statutes. Spots clauses that look standard but create problems in your specific jurisdiction. Can advise whether to sign at all.

The handoff model

AI generates the draft. You download it as a Word file. A lawyer reviews and marks it up. You pay for an hour of attorney time, not the entire drafting process. The AI draft makes attorney review faster and cheaper — the lawyer spends time on judgment, not typing.

When each wins

AI wins here. Lawyer wins there.

Honest about both. Neither is always the right answer.

AI wins

Standard service engagement, moderate value

A freelance project, a consulting engagement, a design contract. Moderate dollar value, standard terms. An AI-generated draft gets you 90% of the way there in five minutes for free. Paying $500 for attorney time on a $3,000 contract doesn't make economic sense.

AI wins

First draft generation

Every contract negotiation starts with someone typing a first draft. Let AI do the typing. The lawyer's time is then spent on review, judgment, and negotiation — not drafting boilerplate.

AI wins

Speed is the constraint

Project starts Monday. Today is Friday. No attorney available until next week. An AI draft gets you a signed agreement before the project starts. That's better than starting with nothing.

Lawyer wins

High-value, high-stakes engagements

Significant money. Major business relationship. Long-term commitment. Regulated industry. These are the situations where the cost of a mistake exceeds the cost of an attorney. Pay for the attorney.

Lawyer wins

Active negotiation with a sophisticated counterparty

If the other side has an attorney and is negotiating hard on terms, you need someone who can negotiate back. AI generates drafts; it doesn't negotiate.

Lawyer wins

Regulated industries

Healthcare, finance, real estate, employment — industries with specific regulatory requirements where a mistake isn't just expensive, it's illegal. AI doesn't know what your specific regulatory context requires.

The handoff model

Use both. In the right order.

The most efficient approach: generate with AI, review with a lawyer for deals that warrant it. The AI draft reduces the attorney's time from two hours to thirty minutes — and cuts your legal bill accordingly.

This isn't a hypothetical. Business lawyers regularly review AI-drafted and template agreements before signing. The first draft isn't the final decision — it's the starting point for review.

The practical approach

  • Draft with BizDraft. Describe the deal, download the Word file. Five minutes, no cost.
  • Review it yourself. Read every clause. The plain-language comments explain what each one does.
  • For high-stakes deals: Send the Word file to an attorney. "Here's a draft — please review and mark up." They review your draft rather than drafting from scratch. Faster, cheaper.
  • For standard engagements: Edit what doesn't fit, sign, proceed. The draft covers the standard protective clauses that most service agreements need.
