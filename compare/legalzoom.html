The key dimensions

Cost, scope, and what you're actually getting.

LegalZoom is a platform. BizDraft is a drafting tool. They serve different needs — sometimes in sequence.

Cost

BizDraft: Free. Always. No account.

LegalZoom: Services range widely. Business formation starts around $0 + state fees. Legal plans (attorney access, document reviews) start at $7.99/month and up. Individual documents are priced per document.

What it does

BizDraft: AI-powered agreement drafting. Describe your deal; get a tailored contract. One thing, done well.

LegalZoom: Business formation (LLC, corporation), registered agent service, trademark filing, estate planning, attorney network, legal plans. A broad legal platform, not a drafting specialist.

Agreement drafting quality

BizDraft: AI-tailored to your specific deal — parties, scope, state, payment structure. Not a template.

LegalZoom: Template-based with attorney review available as an add-on. Templates are comprehensive but standardized; customization requires attorney involvement (additional cost).

When to use which

Different tools for different jobs.

LegalZoom and BizDraft often serve different phases of the same business journey.

BizDraft wins

You need a service agreement now

Freelance agreement, consulting contract, NDA, service agreement — for a specific project starting this week. BizDraft generates a tailored draft in minutes, free, with no account.

BizDraft wins

You want AI tailoring, not template fill

LegalZoom's documents are templates that get filled in. BizDraft describes the deal in plain language and generates clauses that match. The difference shows in non-standard terms.

LegalZoom wins

You're forming a business entity

LLC formation, corporation incorporation, registered agent service — LegalZoom is built for this. BizDraft drafts agreements between parties; it doesn't handle state filings.

LegalZoom wins

You want attorney review included

LegalZoom's legal plans include attorney consultations and document review. If you want a licensed attorney in the loop as part of the service cost, LegalZoom provides that path.

LegalZoom wins

You need trademark or IP filing

Trademark registration, copyright filing, patent assistance — LegalZoom has services for each. BizDraft handles contractual IP clauses, not federal registrations.

Use both

Form your business, then draft agreements

Use LegalZoom to form your LLC. Use BizDraft to draft the service agreements, freelance contracts, and NDAs that follow. They serve sequential phases of the same business.

Honest verdict

Which one should you actually use?

LegalZoom and BizDraft rarely compete for the same job. LegalZoom is for legal infrastructure: forming your business, registering trademarks, getting an attorney. BizDraft is for the agreements that follow: client contracts, service agreements, NDAs, freelance work.

If you need a contract for a project starting next week, BizDraft is the right answer. If you need to form an LLC or file a trademark, LegalZoom is the right answer.

The honest summary

  • Drafting a service agreement or NDA: BizDraft. Free, no account, AI-tailored in minutes.
  • Forming an LLC, registering a trademark: LegalZoom. Purpose-built for legal entity and IP filings.
  • Attorney access included: LegalZoom (with legal plan). BizDraft doesn't offer attorneys.
  • Data storage: LegalZoom stores documents. BizDraft generates locally — you download and keep.
